PRETERITE TENSE
The preterite tense is used when referring to actions that occurred at a fixed
point in time, or in the past that were performed a specific number of times,
or during a specific enclosed period time, or were part of a chain of events.

Regular -ar verbs		example: hablar
é				hablé
aste				hablaste
ó				habló
amos				hablamos
aron				hablaron

Regular -er, -ir verbs		examples: comer, vivir
í				comí, viví
iste				comiste, viviste
ió				comió, vivió
imos				comimos, vivimos
ieron				comieron, vivieron

ser		ir		dar		hacer

IMPERFECT TENSE
The imperfect tense is used to describe something in the past the occurred 
repeatedly or over an extended period of time.

-ar verbs			example: hablar
aba				hablaba
abas				hablabas
aba				hablaba
ábamos				hablábamos
aban				hablaban

-er verbs, -ir verbs		example: vivir
ía				vivía
ías				vivías
ía				vivía
íamos				vivíamos
ían				vivían

CONDITIONAL TENSE
The conditional tense is frequently used to express probability, possibility,
wonder, or conjecture, and is usually translated as would, could, must have or
probably

-ar, -er, -ir verbs			example: comer
ía					comería
ías					comerías
ía					comería
íamos					comeríamos
ían					comerían

FUTURE TENSE
The future tense is used to tell what "will" or "shall" happen.
